ABSA - FATCA

Case study


Design process

1. Empathy: Understanding the user's needs.

2. Define: Identifying the problem.

3. Ideate: Exploring various possible solutions.

4. Prototype: Creating low-scale representations of solutions.

5. Test: Testing each solution and refine.

6. Implement: Implementation of the solution in real-life.

Objective

Tax evasion is a significant global challenge, impacting the economic stability of jurisdictions worldwide. The Tax Reporting Tool was developed to enhance cooperation between tax administrations and enable efficient exchange of information — a critical step in combating tax evasion.

Our objective was to design and develop an intuitive, user-centered interface that simplifies the data reporting process, ensures accuracy, and enhances the overall user experience for both local and international tax authorities.

Solving the problem

To streamline and modernise the FATCA/CRS process, a desktop application was developed to enable tax operators to automatically perform identification, remediation, and classification of client data. Previously a manual and error-prone process, this new FATCA/CRS Reporting Tool introduces basic yet effective automation, significantly improving efficiency and accuracy. The solution enhances the user experience (UX) through simplified workflows and intuitive interfaces, while also ensuring compliance through reliable UI-driven classification and reporting features.

What have I learned

User-Centered Design: Involving users early helped shape the tool's usability and relevance.

Balancing Functionality & Simplicity: Simplifying complex workflows improved user adoption and reduced errors.

Designing for Niche Use Cases: Understanding tax professionals’ needs led to more impactful features.

Working Around Constraints: Navigating technical and legal limitations taught me how to innovate under pressure.

Cross-Team Collaboration: Working with devs and compliance ensured the tool was both usable and compliant.

Rapid Iteration: Small UI tweaks based on testing created big improvements.

Accessibility & Clarity: Clear layout and labels were key for speed and accuracy.
