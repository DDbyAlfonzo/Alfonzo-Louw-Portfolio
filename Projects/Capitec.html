Capitec W.A.R.M

Case study

Design process

1. Empathy:Understanding the user's needs.

2. Define:Identifying the problem.

3. Ideate:Exploring various possible solutions.

4. Prototype:Creating low-scale representations of solutions.

5. Test:Testing each solution and refine.

6. Implement:Implementation of the solution in real-life.

Objective

WhatsApp for Relationship Managers is a Progressive Web Application (PWA) designed to streamline communication between Relationship Managers and their bankers. It enables managers to efficiently close, refer, or escalate banker-submitted requests with speed and ease. Currently, Transactional Bankers and Relationship Managers communicate via social platforms like WhatsApp. However, this often requires Relationship Managers to use their personal accounts or carry bank-issued phones, leading to fragmented communication. With Capitec Bank having no oversight of these interactions, the result is inconsistent service and a lack of accountability. To address this challenge, I was tasked with developing a personalized solution to streamline and secure communication between teams.

What have I learned

UX is never really 'done': There’s always room for refinement and optimization post-launch.

Design with scalability in mind: Planning for growth helped prevent future UX debt.

Constraints fuel creativity: Working within limitations (tech, compliance, timelines) often sparked better solutions.

Feedback is a gift: Constructive critiques improved the final product and strengthened collaboration.

Good design requires alignment: Early alignment with stakeholders saved a lot of time in the final stages.

Clear documentation helps everyone: Minor adjustments in spacing, labeling, and flows had major impacts on user satisfaction.

Small tweaks = big wins: Clear layout and labels were key for speed and accuracy.
