Discovery - Vitality

Case study


Design process

1. Empathy: Understanding the user's needs.

2. Define: Identifying the problem.

3. Ideate: Exploring various possible solutions.

4. Prototype: Creating low-scale representations of solutions.

5. Test: Testing each solution and refine.

6. Implement: Implementation of the solution in real-life.

Objective

I was part of the design team responsible for redesigning the Vitality web page for both desktop and mobile platforms. As part of a larger rebranding effort, Vitality aimed to create a more user-friendly and visually engaging experience to better showcase their products

What have I learned

Empathy is Everything: Understanding the user's perspective is the foundation of great design. Listening, observing, and asking the right questions leads to better solutions.

Function Before Flash: While visuals matter, a design should first and foremost be intuitive and usable. A beautiful interface means little without a seamless user experience.

Iterate Often: The best ideas rarely come from the first draft. Testing, gathering feedback, and refining consistently leads to stronger outcomes.

Collaboration is Key: Working closely with developers, stakeholders, and users has shown me how much stronger a product becomes when diverse perspectives come together.

Details Make the Difference: From microinteractions to typography choices, small details can drastically improve how a product feels and functions.

Stay Curious: UX/UI is constantly evolving. Staying open to learning new tools, patterns, and principles keeps my work fresh and forward-thinking..
