Huawei tracking system

Case study


Design Process

1. Empathy: Understanding the user's needs.
2. Define: Identifying the problem.
3. Ideate: Exploring various possible solutions.
4. Prototype: Creating low-scale representations of solutions.
5. Test: Testing each solution and refine.
6. Implement: Implementation of the solution in real-life.

Objective

The Huawei Tracking System enables real-time monitoring of miners and vehicle activity across mining sites. In addition to operational tracking, the system can also be leveraged to monitor the spread and potential outbreak of viruses like COVID-19.
The system is designed to give site managers full visibility into the movement and location of all personnel and vehicles. It provides insights such as the last known location of individuals, their contact history (critical for contact tracing during health outbreaks), and the duration of time spent in specific zones within the mine.


Solving the problem

To streamline and modernise the FATCA/CRS process, a desktop application was developed to enable tax operators to automatically perform identification, remediation, and classification of client data. Previously a manual and error-prone process, this new FATCA/CRS Reporting Tool introduces basic yet effective automation, significantly improving efficiency and accuracy. The solution enhances the user experience (UX) through simplified workflows and intuitive interfaces, while also ensuring compliance through reliable UI-driven classification and reporting features.
