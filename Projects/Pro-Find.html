Pro-Find

Case study


Design process

1. Empathy: Understanding the user's needs.

2. Define: Identifying the problem.

3. Ideate: Exploring various possible solutions.

4. Prototype: Creating low-scale representations of solutions.

5. Test: Testing each solution and refine.

6. Implement: Implementation of the solution in real-life.

Objective

Many SMMEs relied on traditional recruitment methods to source candidates, often depending on external recruitment agencies or in-house HR teams. While familiar, this approach significantly slowed down the interview and shortlisting process, leading to delays in filling critical roles.
From the job seeker’s perspective, the process was equally frustrating. The requirement to create detailed online profiles and upload numerous supporting documents just to browse available listings created unnecessary friction—ultimately deterring potential applicants and reducing engagement.

User personas

We created user personas for both businesses and applicants, based on their goals, challenges, and behavioral traits. These personas played a key role in shaping the design, helping us empathise with users and make informed decisions. For example, the inclusion of a checklist feature—tailored for business users—was directly inspired by their need to streamline and speed up the application review process.

Journey map

We developed a journey map to visualize the end-to-end experience for both businesses and applicants throughout the job application process. This helped us identify key touchpoints, pain points, and opportunities for improvement.

Wireframes

Low/Mid-fidelity wireframes were created and used for user testing, allowing us to validate the layout, functionality, and overall user experience before moving into development. This helped identify usability issues early and ensured the design aligned with user expectations.

What have I learned

User-Centered Design:: Putting users first led to more intuitive and effective solutions.

Form Meets Function: Balancing aesthetics with usability is essential for a seamless experience.

Collaboration Matters:: Working with stakeholders and developers helped align design with business goals..

Test & Iterate: Early testing and iteration led to better, user-validated outcomes.

Details Count: Small touches—like spacing, typography, and feedback—make a big impact.

Design for Growth: I learned to create scalable, flexible systems that can evolve with the product.
